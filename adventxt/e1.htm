San Gabriel Zoo
San Gabriel, New Mexico 		 
FROM: Dr. Tiger
Victoria Zoo 		 
 RE: Congratulations  

Congratulations! You've helped the police capture a runaway tiger, and identified its subspecies without even laying eyes on the animal itself. That's quite an accomplishment.

The question facing us now, of course, is: Now what? Police Chief Slayton informs me that no one has come forth with a legitimate claim on this tiger. He's consulted with the regional office of the U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service to determine the fate of this tiger. It's not often that a tiger shows up in the United States, unclaimed. The Fish and Wildlife Service has no facility in which to keep it. After consultating with Police Chief Slayton, Fish and Wildlife has decided that the tiger should be donated to an organization which has both the facilities and the expertise to keep non-native animals. Your zoo, being accredited by the American Association of Zoos and Aquariums, would be such an organization.

I know that you haven't any tigers at your zoo currently, nor perhaps a permanent outdoor exhibit area for a tiger. If you choose to keep this tiger, you'll have to design and build an appropriate exhibit which ensures both your tiger's health and visitors' education and enjoyment. I'd be happy to help you design this exhibit.

Now that you've completed Tiger Adventure 1, play Tiger Adventure 2, in which you design a zoo exhibit for this tiger.
