You've done a fine job narrowing down the subspecies possibilities. As you may already suspect, these measurements from Chief Slayton don't help us much in making the final determination. Both the South China and the Sumatran tiger could easily be this size.

There may be another way to make an educated guess about this tiger's subspecies. I suspect that this tiger was captured as a cub--unfortunately, that's all too common today. Recall what you know about both the South China and the Sumatran tigers. Which is more likely to have been captured as a cub and raised by people?

  • The Sumatran tiger, because it's more numerous than the extremely rare South China tiger
