 RE: Tiger Identification  

You think the tiger is a Sumatran, because the Sumatran tiger is more numerous than the extremely rare South China tiger?

That's a good guess. Less than fifty South China tigers are estimated to survive in the wild, compared to 400 to 500 Sumatran tigers. While capturing a wild tiger, even a cub, is not an simple task, it would be easier to catch a Sumatran than a South China tiger.

In many cases, it's impossible to say for certain which subspecies a particular tiger belongs to. Soon, however, we'll have DNA testing, which should finally give us actual proof. For now, Sumatran is a strong educated guess about the identity of this tiger.
