The Tiger Information CenterKey for Tiger on the Loose! Worksheet
on the Tiger Information Center's web site (www.5tigers.org)

1. Who are you? What's your job? You're a zookeeper at the San Gabriel Zoo in New Mexico

2. Where has the tiger escaped from? From the airport customs office.

3. Julia Allende's report about the missing tiger includes a plaster cast of a pugmark (pawprint). Do you think this pugmark was made by the runaway tiger? Why or why not? No -- the pugmark is too small to be from a tiger. It's probably from a mountain lion.

4. Clem Taylor's report included some fur from a wild animal. Do you think this fur was left by the runaway tiger? Why or why not? Yes -- the fur looks like tiger fur, and a runaway tiger would be attracted to a cattle ranch.

5. Jason Cooper may have spotted the runaway tiger in a tree. Do you think his report is reliable evidence of the tiger's whereabouts? Why or why not? No -- unlike domestic house cats, tigers don't climb trees, and they don't prey on small birds.

6. Danny Bence may have spotted the runaway tiger by the river. Do you think his report is reliable evidence of the tiger's whereabouts? Why or why not? No -- tigers enjoy bathing in water and would not have hesitated to follow Danny into the river.

7. Victor Hernandez may have encountered the tiger at his ranch. Do you think his report is reliable evidence of the tiger's whereabouts? Why or why not? No -- the Hernandez ranch is thirty miles away -- too far for the tiger to have traveled in five hours.

8. Where do you think Police Chief Slayton should concentrate his search for the runaway tiger? Why? In the San Gabriel State Forest -- all the sightings have been near the forest. The tiger is probably hiding in the forest and making brief forays in search of food.

9. After you learn the tiger's size and weight from Chief Slayton, what subspecies do you think the tiger is most likely to be? Why? Sumatran, because the Sumatran tiger is more numerous than the extremely rare South China tiger.
