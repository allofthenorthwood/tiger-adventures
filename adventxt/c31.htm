 RE: Runaway Tiger  

Dear Zookeeper,

We've caught the tiger! Thanks to your analysis, my officers and the DNR biologists soon picked up the tiger's trail in the State Forest. They found the runaway in a few hours. Apparently it looked a bit forlorn--hungry, I'm sure, and maybe even a little lonely. The DNR people had no trouble getting close enough to shoot it with a tranquilizer gun. No question about it now--this tiger was raised by people. It was probably glad to be captured.

I received your message about the subspecies. So both people claiming this tiger are lying, are they? At least I can finally get them off my back. In fact, I think I'll call them myself to personally deliver the news.

By the way, my men measured the tiger as soon as they had it in custody. Maybe these numbers will help you make the final analysis about the subspecies. Length: 2.3 meters, weight 100 kg. What do you think? Is it a South China or Sumatran tiger?
