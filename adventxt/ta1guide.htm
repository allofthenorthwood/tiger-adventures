"Tiger on the Loose" Teachers' Guide

What is it? "Tiger on the Loose" is an online interactive adventure designed to teach about tiger biology and behavior.

The Scene: You are a zookeeper in a fictional southwestern city. You are asked to use your expertise to help identify and locate an escaped tiger. To do this, you must learn about tiger subspecies and habits. With help from the interactive Tiger Handbook, you must analyze and map reports of tiger sightings - and decide which are true and which are false.

Who Can Play? Students play independently or in small groups. You could also project it on a screen for a whole class activity.

"Tiger on the Loose" is written for fifth grade and older. There is a significant amount of text in the form of fictional newspaper articles, faxes and police reports. Players are also asked to read maps, rulers and interpret graphics and animations to make their choices.

All our Tiger Adventures are designed to download quickly using Netscape 2 and Exlplorer 3 and above.

How To Play: Encourage students to read and look carefully before making choices. They should consult the Tiger Handbook to acquire the knowledge necessary to identify and locate the missing tiger.

You may want to assign the Tiger on the Loose Worksheet to focus and track your students' progress. Use the teacher key for guidance in correcting or leading a discussion.

Concepts Taught:

Tiger Behavior

  • preferred habitat and prey
  • travelling speed
  • habits (do they like water, climbing trees...)

Tiger Subspecies:

  • Bengal
  • Indochinese
  • South China
  • Siberian
  • Sumatran
  • Extinct subspecies
  • White tigers

Subspecies Differences

  • Size of body and pugmarks
  • Color, including stripe patterns
  • Coat, including fur length

Tiger Ranges

These topics are also explained in topical form, using graphics, animations, and thought-provoking queries, in the Tiger Handbook.

If your students enjoyed "Tiger on the Loose", have them play the sequels: "Zoo Tiger" (Teachers' Guide) and "Save the Tiger" (Teachers' Guide).
