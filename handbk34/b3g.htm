White Tigers


White tigers are not a subspecies of tiger, nor are they albinos (having no color). They are just white-colored Bengal tigers. They have blue eyes, a pink nose, and creamy white fur with chocolate colored stripes. White tigers are only born when two tigers that both carry the rare gene for white coloring mate. White tigers are very rare in the wild, and today they can only be seen in zoos.
