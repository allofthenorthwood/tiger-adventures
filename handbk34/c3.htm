Why do we breed tigers in captivity?

Decades ago, zoos caught wild animals for the public to see. Zoos weren't very good at breeding their captive animals. When their animals died, zoos had to get more from the wild.

Today, better care in zoos has means more zoo babies so they don't have to take as many animals from the wild. Today about 90% of mammals in U.S. zoos were born in zoos.

Zoo breeding can help save endandgered animals like tigers from extinction. Like Noah's ark, zoos can protect a handful of animals whose homes are being destroyed. Zoos from all over the world work together to breed carefully to make sure new animals are strong and healthy.

As zoos get better at breeding tigers, people are starting to think about returning some tigers to the wild.
