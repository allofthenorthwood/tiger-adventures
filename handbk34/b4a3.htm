Size

Northern tigers live in a colder climate and need a larger body to stay warm?

That's correct!

Mammals (animals with fur) which live in cold climates are usually larger than their cousins in warmer climates.

A larger body is capable of producing more heat, which is necessary to survive where it is cold such as in northern Russia. Also, because other mammals such as deer and wild boar also tend to be larger in colder climates, northern tigers must be larger in order to catch and kill their prey.

Northern tigers also have shorter legs and tails so they have less to keep warm. Sumatran tigers, which live where it is very hot, are the smallest tigers and have the longest legs and tails.

Tiger Pugmarks

Tiger pawprints are called pugmarks. Pugmarks range in size according to the size of the animal. Siberian tigers have the largest pugmarks; Sumatran tigers have the smallest. Below is an actual-size Siberian tiger pugmark.
