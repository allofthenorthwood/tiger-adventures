Tiger Behavior

Where in the world do tigers live?

All wild tigers live in Asia. There used to be eight kinds (or subspecies) of tigers, but now there are only five. Three subspecies have gone extinct. Learn more about tiger subspecies in Five Kinds of Tigers.

What kinds of places do tigers like?

Tigers live in thick forests or areas with tall grasses to hide in and plenty of prey to eat. Most kinds of tigers live in the warm jungles of Asia, but some live in the cold forests of the Russian Far East.

Adult tigers live alone and do not share their land or "territory" with other tigers. The size of a tiger's territory depends on how much prey there is to eat. For example, in some parts of India where there is plenty of prey, a male tiger only needs eight to sixty square miles. In Sumatra, where there is less prey, a male tiger may need as much as 150 square miles. And in Siberia, where there is little prey to be found, male tiger territories are as large as 400 square miles.

A female tiger and her young will live together as a group for 2 to 2-3 years, but once the cubs are grown, they will live alone.

How do tigers hunt?

Tigers hunt by stalking (sneaking up) on their prey rather than running after it like lions or cheetahs. Tigers hunt alone. Hunting in a pack would be hard in thick forests and grasslands.

Do tigers like water?

You might think tigers hate water because they are cats. Not only do tigers drink water, but they sometimes like to take cool baths in ponds or rivers. They are also good swimmers.
