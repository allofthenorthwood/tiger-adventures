Name That Tiger:
How to tell kinds of tigers apart.

There are several ways to tell the five kinds of tigers apart. Some tiger subspecies look a lot alike. Unless you know where the tiger lives it can be hard to tell what kind of tiger it is. Even tiger biologists (people who study tigers) cannot always tell tigers apart simply by looking at them.

The main differences are in size, color, and coat.

Size
Northern tigers which live where it is cool are larger than tropical tigers which live where it is warm.

For example, a male Siberian tiger can weigh as much as 300 kilograms (660), compared to a male Sumatran tiger, which may weigh only 100 kilograms (220 pounds).

Why do you think this is true?

  • Tropical tigers have to be smaller to move through the thick jungle
  • More people hunt tigers in the tropics, so they don't live long enough to become as big as northern tigers
  • Northern tigers live in a colder climate and need a larger body to stay warm
  • Tropical tigers eat less because of the heat, so they don't get as big


Color
 The stripes of different tiger subspecies vary in color and pattern. Scientists believe that tiger coloration and stripe patterns evolved as camouflage to help them hide. Tigers which live in thick tropical forests have more stripes and darker stripes than tigers living in grasslands. In the same way, tigers which live in grasslands have darker and denser stripe patterns than tigers living in northern forests.

Coat
 Different tiger subspecies have different kinds of coats. The length and thickness of a tiger's fur has a lot to with where it lives. The males of some tiger subspecies have thick white ruff of hair around their necks.
