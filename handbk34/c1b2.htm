Why do zoo exhibits imitate a tiger's natural environment?

Zoo tigers are often called "ambassadors" to humankind. Their job is to help us learn about the natural world. We will learn more about tigers if zoo exhibits let tigers act like they would in wild. Of course, a fun and and accurate environment may also be an entertaining and provocative home for the tiger too!

So what do zoo exhibits use to imitate the tigers' natural environment?

 

 Water is very important to tigers. All tiger exhibits must have clean drinking water Many exhibits have a pool or stream where the tiger can swim and bathe.
Plants are attractive parts of a zoo exhibit. They can also hide fences, making the exhibit look more natural.


 


Furniture

"Furniture" can stimulate the tiger's activity, making its life more interesting and giving visitors something to watch.
Boomer balls are very tough balls for tigers to play with.

 

Scratching logs encourage tigers to scratch, helping them prevent ingrown claws.
Heat pads are warmed by hot water running through underground pipes. The warm pads attracts tigers in cold weather.

 
Heat pads attract tigers to specific areas of the exhibit
 

Scent trails can renew the tiger's interest in its surroundings by introducing new smells from time to time. These may include catnip, perfumes and spices. The scent trail may lead to a food reward and encourages the tiger to be more active, which is more fun for the visitor.
