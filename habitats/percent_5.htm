Five percent is correct!

A deer population can actually lose about 12% of its members and remain healthy. However, some deer will die from other causes, so—as a tiger—you want to eat, at most, 5% of the population.

If you eat 33 deer, and they can be only 5% of the deer population, how many deer must there be?


Six hundred and sixty deer! So how much land do 660 deer need? One square kilometer of land can typically support 55 deer—so 660 deer would need twelve square kilometers of habitat.

So you, one Bengal tiger, also need twelve square kilometers (about 4.5 square miles) of land to survive.*

Many people think that poaching is the biggest threat to tigers. But the loss of valuable habitat is just as big of a threat to tigers. Without twelve square kilometers of good habitat, a tiger has a very hard time surviving!

In the Namallai Forest, highways are slicing through the forest, reducing the amount of good habitat for deer and tigers.

In Kaziranga, water pollution is a growing problem, since nearby tea plantations often release pesticides into the swamps. Recent floods have killed hundreds of animals, who had nowhere to run to outside the park boundaries.

How can you find out more about tiger survival? How can you get involved?

  • A tiger conservationist named Bitthu Saghal has written several letters explaining the situation in India and how you can help the tigers there. Read them and learn more!
  • A massive World Bank-funded mining expansion is threatening the habitat for around 30 per cent of the world's remaining wild tigers. Learn about it, and how to take action to help stop it!
  • And find out more ways to help!

You can also try your luck hunting for your survival—in the same habitat as you chose the first time, or a different one!

*Of course, tigers eat more than deer, so their habitat would also have to support many wild boars and other animals. But the amount of land would still be about the same.
