"Save the Tiger!" Teachers' Guide

What is it? "Save the Tiger!" is an online interactive adventure designed to teach about captive tiger breeding, the role of zoos in the conservation of endangered species, and the genetics of breeding wild animals.

The Scene: You are a zookeeper in a fictional southwestern city. You must made decisions about the breeding plans for a recently-captured Sumatran tiger (acquired in "Tiger on the Loose," and for which you designed an exhibit in "Zoo Tiger"). You must debate the fate of the tiger with the proprietor of a roadside zoo. He wants to breed your tiger with his female, and you must deal with him throughout the game. You must also select the best mate to breed with your tiger, based on genetic and other considerations.

Who Can Play? Students can play independently or in small groups. You could also project it on a screen for a whole class activity.

"Save the Tiger!" is written for fifth grade and older. There is a significant amount of text in the form of fictional newspaper articles, letters and memos. Players participate in a mock-debate about captive breeding of endangered species. They must also learn some fairly difficult information about genetics to finish the game successfully.

All our Tiger Adventures are designed to download quickly using Netscape 2 and Exlplorer 3 and above.

How To Play: Encourage students to read and look carefully before making choices. The Tiger Handbook will provide the necessary information construct a successful and healthy exhibit for your tiger. The Tiger Handbook also challenges players to consider the role of zoos in our society as well as how to balance the needs of the animals with the desires of the public.

You may want to assign the Save the Tiger Worksheet to focus and track your students' progress. Use the teacher key for guidance in correcting or leading a discussion.

Concepts Taught:

  • Captive breeding as a way to save endangered species
  • Genetics of captive breeding
    • Genetic Diversity
    • Kinship
    • Inbreeding

If your students enjoyed "Save the Tiger", have them play the prequels "Tiger on the Loose" (Teachers' Guide) and "Zoo Tiger" (Teachers' Guide).

These topics are also explained in topical form, using graphics, animations, and thought-provoking queries, in the Tiger Handbook.
