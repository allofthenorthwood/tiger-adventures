Name the Tiger Contest


First prize: Tiger poster by Tom Brakefield		 Winning entry:
Jambi
Submitted by Kate Nichols.
Jambi is a province in Sumatra, where our tiger came from. As Kate notes, it's "a good, playful name for a tiger that was raised by people."

Honorable Mentions (in alphabetical order):

Atjeh, submitted by Helen Hille. Atjeh is another province in Sumatra.

Indah Bung, submitted by James R. Beidle. Indah Bung means "beautiful friend" in Indonesian.

Kerinci, submitted by both Tom Curran and Marlene McManus. Kerinci is the highest mountain peak in Sumatra.

Kuching, submitted by Colin Haywood. Kuching is the Malay word for cat.

Merantau, submitted by Shannon Pack. Merantau means "go abroad," which is just what our tiger did.

Pemburu, submitted by Lisa Spain. Pemburu means "hunter" in Indonesian.

The winner will receive an autographed poster of a tiger (shown above) by famous wildlife photographer Tom Brakefield. Everyone whose submission was selected as an honorable mention will receive a tiger calender.

Thank you to everyone who entered the contest!
