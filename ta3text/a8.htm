"Hello! I'm a zookeeper here and I'd like to explain to you all some things that Don Wilson hasn't.

"Don Wilson may have found that his tiger is Sumatran, just as ours is, but that doesn't mean we can breed them. The purpose of zoos' captive management and breeding programs is to build a "genetic insurance policy" for the future. Someday the wild tiger population may become so small and inbred that it cannot survive without help. We may have to strengthen it by reintroducing new genes into the wild population--either the genetic code alone or the animals themselves.

"So the point today is not to breed as many tigers as we can, but to maintain as much genetic diversity as possible among captive tigers. We may know that Toni the Tiger is Sumatran, but we don't know who her parents, much less her grandparents, were. Without knowing her lineage, and her genetic history, we don't know how she would affect the gene pool of all captive tigers. We might also unknowingly breed her with a close relative. No, we must make every effort to make the genetic pool as diverse as possible. The survival of the world's tigers may depend on it."
