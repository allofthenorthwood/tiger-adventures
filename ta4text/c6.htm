C6 Going to Lhasa

I caught the next airplane to Lhasa. The flight over the Himalaya Mountains was pretty rough. I was glad I had skipped breakfast.

The views were incredible, but they made me dizzy. To distract myself, I opened Dr. Tiger's handbook and read about Tibet:

Tibet is often called the "Roof of the World," since it is located on the world's highest plateau. Most of Tibet lies more than 12,000 feet above sea level. Tibet was once the most geographically isolated country in the world. In 1959 Tibet lost its independence when China took control and renamed it Xizang (sit-sang). Many Tibetans still resist Chinese control, but resistance becomes more difficult as China strengthens its economic links with Tibet.
