"There are some good projects happening in Asia," Dr. Tiger said. "Support them by sending money, if you can. If you can't send money, write them a letter saying how much you appreciate the work they're doing. (But don't ask for them to write you back--they've got too much to do already!)

"For example, The Wildlife Protection Society of India is recognised as the premier non-goverment investigation agency working against tiger poachers and traders. It gathers information and works with enforcement authorities to apprehend wildlife criminals. It aims to avert the Indian wildlife crisis through investigation and enforcement, legal support, education and training, and research.

"Global Tiger Patrol funds projects in India that strengthen the front line in tiger conservation and protection by providing much needed equipment and NGO (non-government organisation) expertise to the forest guards.

"Also, the TigerLink Awards recognize unsung heroes in India who have gone above and beyond the call of duty in their efforts to stop poaching and save the tiger. Follow the lead of the Tiger Information Center, which recently sponsored a TigerLink Award, by sending your own donation.
