We flew over the vast Gobi desert on the way to Beijing, the capital of China. Since I had never been to China, I checked Dr. Tiger's fact sheet about the country.

China has one of the world's oldest civilizations, dating to 2200 B.C. It is currently the world's most populous country, with 1.2 billion people. Its recent population growth has put many demands on the natural environment.

As a result, tigers have suffered greatly. Two subspecies of tiger—the Siberian and the South China tiger—live in China, but both are endangered. There are estimated to be only fifty Siberian tigers left in China. There are probably only a few dozen—if any—South China tigers still in the wild.

The Chinese have long revered the tiger as a symbol of strength. Ancient Chinese medical texts recommended various tiger body parts as the cure for many illnesses and health concerns.
