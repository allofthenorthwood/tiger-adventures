"Tiger parts?" the shopkeeper repeated. He looked me up and down. No doubt few foreigners visited his little shop.

"I'm sorry," he finally said, "but it is illegal to sell anything made of tiger parts."

"Are you sure?" I asked. "I've heard that they're very popular."

"No, no," he insisted. "I have never sold any medicines with tiger parts, and I cannot help you. Now please leave my shop."

I sighed and left. Down the street was another small pharmacy. Maybe I might have more success if I tried a more subtle approach.

I entered the other drugstore and approached the pharmacist.

"I'm having spasms of pain in my back," I said. "Do you have anything that might help me?"
