Ranjit laughed. "Crack down on poaching? You have nothing to worry about. There is no danger from the government. They are incompetent and corrupt. A small bribe here and there takes care of any problems."

Sanjoy's jaw tightened, but he said nothing. I smiled privately. Sanjoy was one Forest Service ranger who couldn't be bribed. I was confident that there were many more.

As proof, I saw four police officers waiting outside their station as Sanjoy turned up the drive.

"Betrayer!" Ranjit cried. He pulled out his crude gun and waved it at me and Sanjoy. Gopal, shouting, pulled out a stubby rifle from beneath the folds of his clothing. I shouted back at Ranjit and Gopal, but I had nothing to defend myself with.

The police officers wasted no time. They dragged Ranjit and Gopal out and slapped handcuffs around their wrists. Two led the poachers into the station.

Sanjoy swung open the trunk and showed the rest of us the burlap sack. "Here is the evidence," he declared. "Two tiger skins."

The policemen examined the skins and then turned to us. "You know you are crazy people," one told us. "Even we police cannot go into the neighborhoods where these men live. It is very dangerous."

Sanjoy slapped me on the back. "Yes, I was also worried for a moment."

"I'm sorry if I endangered the operation," I said. "I'm used to asking questions, not holding my tongue."

I sighed as the excitement of the evening caught up with me. I told Sanjoy, "This whole adventure has worn me out.

..."I'm going home."

..."But I'm not done asking questions yet."
