"Tracking the Tiger Trade" Teachers' Guide

What is it? "Tracking the Tiger Trade" is an online interactive adventure designed to teach about threats facing wild tigers today, specifically poaching, the trade in tiger parts, and the use of tiger parts in traditional Chinese medicine.

The Scene: You are a journalist sent to India to investigate tiger poaching. You go undercover with an Indian Forest officer to catch two tiger poachers. You then follow the tiger trade into Tibet to the final market in China. It concludes with suggestions for ways to help save tigers from poaching. One suggestion is to write an article about the tiger trade. You can use this as a class or individual assignment to teach research and writing skills.

Who Can Play? Students can play independently or in small groups. You could also project it on a screen for a whole class activity.

"Tracking the Tiger Trade" is written for fifth grade and older. There is a fair amount of text in the narrative.

All our Tiger Adventures are designed to download quickly using Netscape 2 and Exlplorer 3 and above.

How To Play: Encourage students to read and look carefully before making choices. The Tiger Handbook will provide the necessary information construct a successful and healthy exhibit for your tiger. The Tiger Handbook also challenges players to consider the role of zoos in our society as well as how to balance the needs of the animals with the desires of the public.

You may want to assign the Tracking the Tiger Trade Worksheet to focus and track your students' progress. Use the teacher key for guidance in correcting or leading a discussion.

Concepts Taught:

  • Tiger Poaching, and why poor people in India kill tigers
  • Use of tigerparts in traditional Chinese medicine
  • Geography of Asia, specifically India, Tibet and China

These topics are also explained in topical form, using graphics, and thought-provoking queries, in the Tiger Handbook.

If your students enjoyed "Tracking the Tiger Trade", have them play our other Tiger Adventures: "Tiger on the Loose" (Teachers' Guide), "Zoo Tiger," (Teachers' Guide), and "Save the Tiger" (Teachers' Guide).
