"Shahtoosh shawls are the best in the world," Tsering said proudly. "The wool is so fine you can pass a shawl through a ring! And they are rare, very very rare. You must kill the antelope before you can shear it. And you need three antelopes to make one shawl."

I recoiled in shock. The Chiru antelope was undoubtedly a protected species, making trade in its wool prohibited by international treaties. The tiger trade was linked to illegal trade in other endangered wildlife!

But I had not yet reached the end of the trail. What was the final destination for the tiger bones?

"I don't know," I said dubiously. "I think I can get much more for my bones if I take them directly to the market in...

 ...Mexico City."

...New York."

...Beijing."

...Nairobi."
