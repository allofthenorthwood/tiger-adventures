RE:Exhibit plans

I had better caution you now that your plan will almost certainly not be approved by the AZA Tiger SSP. That type of bare, cave-like exhibit is termed a "bear grotto" and is no longer considered appropriate in a modern zoo, for many reasons. The gunnite floor can become very hot, especially in a southern state such as New Mexico. There's nowhere for the tiger to be alone or away from visitors. Furthermore, the exhibit does not resemble the tiger's natural habitat. The hard gunnite floor will give the tiger sore feet and cause abrasions on its pads. And there is no comfortable area for sleeping (remember, tigers sleep 80% of the day).

Perhaps you should review the plans and pick another.
