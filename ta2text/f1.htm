TO: Zookeeper
San Gabriel Zoo
San Gabriel, New Mexico
FROM: Anne Zimmerman
Director, San Gabriel Zoo
San Gabriel, New Mexico
RE: New veterinarian

Congratulations--the Board is so impressed with our progress with the new exhibit that it has approved funds to hire a second veterinarian to help Dr. Kane care for the tiger and our other animals in our collection. We've already received many applications for the job. I've asked several of the most qualified applicants to briefly describe how they would care for the tiger. Please make the final hiring decision based on their response.

And remember, we need to move quickly. The tiger exhibit is nearly completed, and opening day will be here soon.

 

 

 

 Dr. A.J. Miller

"I will feed the tiger only food which has been specifically prepared for large zoo cats and which closely adheres to scientific knowledge about tiger diets. I will feed it nightly, upon its return to the night room, so I can monitor its health on a daily basis. Only in this way can we be assured of maintaining the tiger's health and ensuring its long life."

 Dr. Arnold Schwartz

"Tigers are wild animals, not domesticated pets. I believe that they know better than any veterinarian when they are hungry and when they are not. Therefore, I allow the tiger to eat when it is hungry. I will feed it actual animal carcasses, so it can practice its hunting skills--and demonstrate them for zoo visitors."

 Dr. Sara Cruz

"I would give the tiger both commercially-prepared food and animal carcasses. The tiger would receive its primary meal at night, in its holding room, so I have a chance to monitor its appetite--and thus its health--on a daily basis. On occasion, I would also give it an animal carcass in the exhibit, for the benefit of both zoo visitors and the tiger itself."
