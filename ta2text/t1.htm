Tiger Escapes Zoo Exhibit

Second escape this year confined to zoo grounds

Wilson Accuses Zoo of Incompetence

Donald T. Wilson, owner of Don Wilson's Wildlife Wonderland, said that Zoo officials were incompetent and should not have been given custody of the Sumatran tiger which escaped yesterday from the zoo.
Wilson, who has been vying for custody of the tiger for months, said that the tiger's escape proves that he has been right from the start. "I've been saying for months that I can provide a better home for the tiger. The only difference is that now everybody knows I'm right. What a waste of taxpayer money. I just hope that people come to their senses and give me the tiger before any more money is spent on the Zoo's incompetent attempt at a tiger enclosure."
Zoo officials maintained that their exhibit design needed only minor alterations. "Don Wilson can talk all he wants," said a Zoo employee who asked to remain anonymous. "But he's running a circus out there, not a real zoo. I just hope the people of San Gabriel see the difference and continue to support us."

A Sumatran tiger escaped yesterday from the new exhibit which had been designed and built expressly for it, and roamed the zoo grounds for fifteen hours before it was captured and returned to its holding room. No one was injured in the incident, though Zoo officials are still checking to be sure that no other zoo animal fell prey to the wandering tiger.
