RE:Sumatran tiger

What a surprise to receive your letter! I consulted the International Tiger Studbook and the records kept by the Indonesian Zoological Parks Association (PKBSI). Your tiger and this transponder are one and the same. The tiger was born almost three years ago at Bukkitinggi Zoo in West Sumatra, Indonesia. Zoo officials reported it and its brother stolen when they were six months old. I don't know how it got out of Indonesia, but some unscrupulous animal dealer must have stolen it from the zoo.

Because this tiger is registered in the International Tiger Studbook, we know its pedigree. Its father was born and raised in the Bukkitinggi Zoo and its mother was wild-caught. It is a very valuable animal!

I have consulted with officials at the PKBSI. Since the tiger is already in the United States, they have decided to transfer ownership of the tiger to the SSP. Do you have a place to keep and breed this tiger? If so, we will of course want to inspect and approve the exhibit. If not, we'll send it to another AZA-accredited zoo. It is a very valuable animal and we have high hopes of breeding it and improving the genetic pool of North American captive tigers.
