Zoo Unveils New Tiger Exhibit

Opening day a success despite complaints

Wilson Hopes to Breed Tigers

Donald T. Wilson, owner of Don Wilson's Wildlife Wonderland, expressed hope yesterday that his captive tiger and the tiger at the zoo would be bred. "It's a win-win situation," he said. "They've got a male tiger, I've got a female tiger. Why not let nature take her course?"
Wilson, who fought zoo officials for custody of the tiger, says he feels no ill will against the zoo. "Of course they wanted the tiger--they've got to attract tourists to the zoo. I just wish they were more devoted to educating our young people about wildlife. But by breeding our tigers, I think we can make a positive step forward together. I sincerely hope it comes to pass."

The San Gabriel Zoo unveiled its new exhibit yesterday to house the Sumatran tiger which briefly roamed the area earlier this year.
According to Zoo officials, the tiger enclosure incorporates the best and most current thinking in zoo exhibit design. "We've provided the tiger with a natural environment and many things to enrich its life, such as scratching logs and heat pads, and of course a healthy diet. I hope everyone comes out to the zoo to get to know our newest resident."
However, some visitors to the zoo yesterday said the tiger did not appear as happy as they would have liked. "He looked so hot," said Danny Bence, age 10.


Sumatran tiger enjoys its new home at the San Gabriel Zoo.

"He was laying there on the rock with his tongue hanging out like he was gonna die." Other visitors also commented on the tiger's appearance. Art Ocampo, 42, said, "He looked like he was about to faint from sunstroke, or something."
Zoo officials denied that the tiger not in the best of health, but Victor Sanchez, an employee of El Paso Zoo who came to San Gabriel for the occasion, was concerned about the lack of water and shade. "Tigers love water," he said, "but there aren't any pools or creeks in this exhibit for the tiger to bathe in. No wonder he's hot."
