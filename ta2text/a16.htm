Tiger Exhibit Plan Attacked

"Puts tiger in jail," say critics

Name the Tiger!

The San Gabriel Gazette is sponsoring a contest to name the tiger which was recently captured after escaping from airport customs. The winner will receive an autographed poster of a Sumatran tiger by renowned wildlife photographer Tom Brakefield.
The winning entry will be selected by a panel of judges and Zoo staff chosen by the Gazette. Submit your entry by e-mail by April 25, 1997 (note: sorry, the contest is over). Be sure to include the following information: your name and email address, your suggestion for the tiger's name, and your reason for giving the tiger this name. Remember that the tiger came from Sumatra, an island in Indonesia. Names drawing on Indonesian or Sumatran culture, language or natural history have a better chance of winning the contest.

At last night's City Council meeting, a storm of protest greeted the plans for the new exhibit to house the tiger which recently ran loose in the San Gabriel area.
The plan calls for a small, concrete-floored exhibit measuring sixty feet by thirty feet. According to Zoo officials the plan was chosen because it will give zoo visitors a close-up view of the tiger. It will also be low-cost, easy to maintain and clean.
Critics, however, denounced the exhibit. Mark Fahey, president of the San Gabriel Animal Humane Society, said, "You might as well put the tiger in jail. It's a tiny, cramped, inhumane cell, and I don't think the people of San Gabriel will stand for it."

A cage similar to that chosen by the San Gabriel Zoo for its new tiger exhibit.

In an unprecedented move, Mayor Janet Roebuck announced that the City Council will review the decision, and perhaps take a closer look at other management actions by the zoo.
Donald T. Wilson, owner of Don Wilson's Wildlife Wonderland, also criticized the plan. "I've said again and again that the zoo is not the place for this tiger. My Wildlife Wonderland already has a well-designed tiger area. It's just common sense that the tiger come here."
