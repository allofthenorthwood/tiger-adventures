RE: Proposals for Tiger Exhibit Design

Please find below two proposals for the design of your new tiger exhibit.

Comments: This exhibit would offer all zoo visitors a good, nose-to-nose view of the tiger. No one would be able to complain that the tiger is too far away, or hiding behind the vegetation. In addition, the concrete floor ensures a clean and hygenic exhibit. There would be no chance of dangerous organisms or parasites contaminating the exhibit. In addition, we have made provisions for moving the tiger between the exhibit and the off-exhibit holding area and night rooms.

Would you like to choose this design?

 

Comments: This design provides a safe, secure, yet attractive enclosure for the tiger. By retaining the existing cottonwood trees, this exhibit would offer zoo visitors a natural, wooded border, while also concealing the five-meter (16-foot) perimeter fence. Additional vegetation hides the wire fencing from visitors' views. Zookeepers would appreciate the provisions to move the tiger between the exhibit and the holding area and night rooms, which are concealed underground.

Would you like to choose this design?

TO: Anne Zimmerman, Director
San Gabriel Zoo
San Gabriel, New Mexico
FROM: Sam O'Connell
Architect
O'Connell Group
Albuqueque, New Mexico
RE: Tiger Exhibit Design

We have enclosed two different proposals for your new tiger exhibit. We hope you find one of them satisfactory.

Comments: In this plan, we propose a low-maintenance, high-visibility exhibit. The tiger has a large area in which to roam, yet it will always remain in sight of zoo visitors. The floor is gunnite, which resembles rock but is as tough and sturdy as concrete. It is also easy to clean, forestalling the formation of pathogens which might make the tiger ill.

Would you like to choose this design?

 

Comments: This design is spacious and attractive. The dirt substrate (ground) and ample vegetation recreate the tiger's natural habitat while educating zoo visitors about the tiger's ecosystem. A moat ensures that the tiger will not escape, yet brings visitors face-to-face with the tigers. Vegetation screening blocks the fencing from view. Narrow chutes will help zookeepers guide the tiger between the exhibit and the underground off-exhibit night rooms.

Would you like to choose this design?
